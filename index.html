§01 ABOUT / SUBJECT

Technology professional who took a postgraduate detour through public policy — and never quite came back.

Three decades transforming cities through technology and data-driven solutions in sustainable mobility and energy transition. The unusual mix — a technology background plus a PG in Public Policy — is the whole point: most urban problems aren't blocked by ignorance, they're blocked by the gap between people who can read systems and people who can read the bylaw.

Pioneering Urban Mobility

Architect of AltMo, a gamified mobility intelligence platform for climate-friendly cities. AltMo's data drove a 342% increase in cycling and walking infrastructure allocation in Bengaluru's Comprehensive Master Plan, established 20+ km of dedicated cycle tracks, and won Silvassa Smart City an Excellence in Innovation award. Today, AltMo helps corporations meet ESG goals while advancing sustainable urban transport.

Recognition & Global Impact

The Relief Riders initiative during COVID-19 earned the 2021 UN World Bicycle Day Special Award and a nomination for the 2022 Nobel Peace Prize. The network spread across 12 cities with 725 volunteers, demonstrating community-led mobility at crisis scale.

Thought Leadership

Author of Break the Block: Lessons from Bengaluru's Rail Battle — a field guide chronicling a decade-long citizen campaign that brought commuter rail to the city, distilled into 90 lessons for change-makers. As host of OoruLabs, India's leading urban affairs podcast, the long-form conversations sit with policymakers, activists, and planners on what actually moves Indian cities.

Currently

  • Co-Founder, Urban Morph — urban consulting on sustainable cities, mobility, and climate-resilient design across India.
  • Strategy Lead, Rainmatter Foundation — thesis-based investing for urban sustainability, waste, energy transition, and sustainable materials.
  • Bicycle Mayor of Bengaluru — cycling infrastructure and sustainable mobility advocacy.

Also associated with WattsNext Energy, Bioman Technologies, and as visiting/adjunct faculty at Kautilya School of Public Policy and Mount Carmel College.
