§01 BUILT FOR THE PUBLIC

Five things I built that you can use right now: free, no login, open source.

FIG. 01 / TOOL OPEN SOURCE

mdshare

Markdown sharing with the widest surface area: paste in the browser, edit from Obsidian, commit from VS Code, or drive it from Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, Windsurf, Codex & Gemini CLIs via MCP. Four permission tiers, inline threaded comments anchored to text, real-time WebSocket sync, and instant link revocation. Free, no-login, open source.

mdshare.live
FIG. 02 / TOOL OPEN ATLAS · NO SIGNUP

bharatlas

India's open atlas. 100+ map layers, free to explore. Administrative boundaries from state down to village, wards for 30+ cities, plus rivers, forests, highways, groundwater, floods and seismic zones. Slice any layer and download it as GeoJSON, Shapefile, Parquet or PMTiles, or drop your own geo file and share it by link. Open licences, queryable from AI tools via MCP, no signup, no tracking.

bharatlas.com
FIG. 03 / TOOL MIT · NO LOGIN

Irrational

A field guide to the mistakes your mind makes. Describe a decision and Irrational writes an adversarial prompt that argues the other side, refracting your choice through 22 cognitive biases: sunk cost, anchoring, overconfidence. Your own AI runs the audit; nothing is stored. Works as an MCP tool. MIT, no login.

irrational.pages.dev
FIG. 04 / TOOL OPEN SKY · NO LOGIN

Mugilu

The open sky of India. Name any place and Mugilu reads what the air is doing to you right now: AQI with the full pollutant breakdown and years of life lost, heat and wet-bulb stress, wind, fog, dust, crop-burn smoke, UV, and any live NDMA or IMD warning overhead. Search in Indian languages or use your location, no map needed. Open source, no login, queryable from AI tools via MCP.

mugilu.live
FIG. 05 / TOOL CC-BY · NO LOGIN

twelveswaras

Shazam for raagas. Hold your phone to Carnatic music and twelveswaras names the raaga: the top three matches with confidence, the tonic pinned from the tanpura drone, and plain-language cues for training your own ear. Audio is read on the spot and never stored. Free, no login, open source, a CC-BY public good.

twelveswaras.com
§02 STORIES / INVESTIGATIONS

Data-driven investigations into how India actually works: forests, farmers, livelihoods, rights.

Biodiversity · Governance · 2026 The Register No One Reads India built 2.72 lakh People's Biodiversity Registers to put villages in charge of the nature around them. Two have been formally approved.
pbr-story.surge.sh
Rural India · Sustainability · 2026 The Ground Beneath the Report Card India grades 2.6 lakh villages on how they're doing today, not whether they can last. Its showcase grain belt is also the fastest at draining its own water.
canitlast.surge.sh
Wildlife · Human Conflict · 2026 The Uncounted India logs over 100,000 human-wildlife conflict incidents a year across 88 species, yet keeps a national death count for just two.
the-uncounted.surge.sh
Forests · CFR Rights · 2026 Whose Forest Is It? India mapped 3.46 crore hectares of community forest that local communities could govern. Less than 3% has been recognised.
whose-forest.surge.sh
Agriculture · FPOs The Hollow Collective India helped small farmers form nearly 50,000 companies they own together. Just 4% employ even one paid manager.
fpo-story.surge.sh
Livelihoods · Women · SHGs The Ground They Stand On India's self-help groups are the world's largest financial-inclusion effort: 9 crore women across 8.6 million groups. Yet even the best-served state leaves 71% stuck at saving, never reaching credit.
shg-story.surge.sh
§03 ABOUT / SUBJECT

Technology professional who took a detour through public policy, and never quite came back.

Three decades transforming cities through technology and data-driven solutions in sustainable mobility and energy transition. The unusual mix (a technology background plus a PG in Public Policy) is the whole point: most urban problems aren't blocked by ignorance, they're blocked by the gap between people who can read systems and people who can read the bylaw.

The work itself is in the cards: the open tools up in §01, and the platforms, initiative, book and podcast just below in §04. What stays here is where the time goes now.

Currently

  • CEO, Rainmatter Foundation. Enabling the increase of climate-resilient places across urban, rural and the wild.
  • Co-Founder, Urban Morph. Full-stack, AI-native urban-tech venture studio. Ships AltMo and SyntheSYS.
  • Bicycle Mayor of Bengaluru. Cycling infrastructure and sustainable mobility advocacy.

Also associated with WattsNext Energy, Bioman Technologies, and as visiting/adjunct faculty at Kautilya School of Public Policy and Mount Carmel College.
